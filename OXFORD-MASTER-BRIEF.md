# City Market Brief — Commercial Property + Lending

## City: Oxford, Oxfordshire

Oxford is a knowledge-economy commercial market: tight planning, very limited new commercial stock, strong life-sciences and tech demand, and a tourism overlay that drives unusually high hospitality and retail values per sq ft. For commercial mortgages this means a market dominated by **owner-occupier and refinance flow** rather than speculative investment — the supply is too tight for fresh investment churn at scale. Asset values are high, LTVs typically conservative (60–70%), and lender appetite skews toward the strongest-covenant deals. The Oxford Science Park / Begbroke / Harwell adjacency drives a discrete lab-and-research property cluster with bespoke financing requirements; Westgate, Oxford Castle Quarter and Headington's hospital-adjacent strip drive retail and leisure deal flow.
